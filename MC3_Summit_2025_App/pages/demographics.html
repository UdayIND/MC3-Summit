    Age Distribution Insights

    Monroe County maintains a healthy age structure with 18% under 18, 67% working age, and 15% over 65. This distribution supports economic vitality while indicating growing needs for senior services.

    • Youth Population: Strong retention due to local opportunities
    • Working Age: Largest segment supports economic growth
    • Senior Population: Growing segment requires service planning

    Income Growth Analysis

    Monroe County has experienced steady income growth outpacing inflation, with median household income rising from $48,750 to $59,800 (2015-2023).

    • Growth Rate: 2.3% annually above inflation
    • Economic Mobility: Strong job market supports income gains
    • Income Gap: Difference between median and mean indicates some inequality

    Geographic Distribution Patterns

    Population density varies significantly across Monroe County, with urban core showing highest density around Bloomington and IU campus, while rural areas maintain lower density patterns.

    • Urban Core: Highest density near university and downtown
    • Suburban Growth: Expanding residential development
    • Rural Areas: Stable low-density agricultural/residential mix

    Education and Demographics Relationship

    Monroe County shows strong educational attainment across age groups, with particularly high rates of college completion among working-age adults.

    • Higher Education: 45% of adults have bachelor's degrees or higher
    • Age Patterns: Younger adults show higher completion rates
    • Economic Impact: Education levels correlate with income growth

    Data Sources & Methodology

    Population Data

    • U.S. Census Bureau - Decennial Census (2010, 2020)
    • American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates (2015-2023)
    • Census tract boundary files (TIGER/Line)
    • Annual population estimates (Census Bureau)

    Income Analysis

    • ACS Table S1901 - Income in Past 12 Months
    • ACS Table S1902 - Mean Income in Past 12 Months
    • ACS Table S1903 - Median Income in Past 12 Months
    • All income data adjusted to 2023 dollars using CPI-U

    Educational Attainment

    • ACS Table S1501 - Educational Attainment
    • Population 25 years and over by education level
    • Cross-tabulated with age, race, and income data
    • Graduation rates from Indiana Department of Education

    Geographic Mobility

    • ACS Table B07009 - Geographical Mobility by Educational Attainment
    • Migration patterns in and out of Monroe County
    • Residence 1 year ago analysis
    • Cross-reference with employment and education data
    Note: All data represents the most recent available information through 2024. Margin of error varies by estimate; see individual dataset documentation for specific confidence intervals.
