Educational Performance Trends

High School Graduation Rates

A Decade of Educational Progress: The Graduation Success Story

Our analysis reveals a remarkable transformation: Monroe County's graduation rate has climbed from 78.2% in 2015 to an impressive 92.5% in 2024, representing a 14.3% improvement that consistently outpaces both state (85.1%) and national (84.6%) averages.

Most significantly, we've achieved substantial progress in closing equity gaps. Hispanic students showed the most dramatic improvement (18.2% increase), while students from low-income families saw their graduation rate rise from 71.4% to 82.8%. The introduction of wraparound support services in 2019 and expanded mental health programs correlate directly with these gains.

Data Sources: Indiana Department of Education, Monroe County Community School Corporation

Achievement Gap Analysis

Breaking Barriers: The Achievement Gap Challenge

The data reveals both progress and persistent challenges: While overall reading proficiency has improved to 74.2% and math to 71.8%, significant gaps remain. Students eligible for free/reduced lunch score 23 percentage points lower in reading and 26 points lower in math compared to their more affluent peers.

However, targeted interventions are working: Schools implementing intensive tutoring programs saw a 15% reduction in achievement gaps over three years. Our analysis shows that every additional hour of individualized support correlates with a 3.2% improvement in test scores, with the greatest impact among students furthest behind.

Data Sources: ISTEP+ Results, ILEARN Assessment Data

School Enrollment Patterns

Enrollment Dynamics: Understanding Community Choice

Monroe County's education landscape is evolving: Total K-12 enrollment has stabilized at 18,567 students after declining 8.2% from 2018-2021 due to demographic shifts and pandemic impacts. Public school enrollment now represents 89.3% of all students, with charter schools gaining 12% market share since 2020.

The data reveals concerning equity patterns: 47.3% of students qualify for free/reduced lunch, while special education enrollment has increased 15% since 2019, indicating greater identification and support needs. Early childhood enrollment (Pre-K) shows the strongest growth at 23%, suggesting increased recognition of early intervention importance.

Data Sources: S1401 School Enrollment Census Data
