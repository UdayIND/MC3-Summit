Social Services Data & Trends

Food Assistance Programs

The Hidden Hunger Crisis: Children at Risk

Food insecurity affects 1 in 4 Monroe County children: Despite economic growth, 24.7% of children under 18 experience food insecurity, compared to 16.3% of adults. SNAP participation increased 28% since 2019, serving 8,234 households monthly, while WIC supports 3,456 participants, primarily mothers and young children.

School meal programs provide critical support: 47.3% of students qualify for free/reduced lunch, with participation rates reaching 89.3% - indicating both need and effective program delivery. Our analysis shows children receiving consistent school meals score 12% higher on standardized tests, demonstrating the clear link between nutrition security and educational outcomes.

Data Sources: S2201 SNAP Data, WIC Program Data, School Corporation

Housing & Financial Support

Housing Stability Crisis: The 18-Month Wait

Housing assistance demand far exceeds supply: The housing voucher waitlist averages 18 months, with 1,247 families currently waiting. Only 23% of eligible families receive assistance, while emergency housing requests increased 45% since 2022. TANF serves just 892 families monthly, down 12% from 2020 despite growing need.

Prevention programs show promise: Our new eviction prevention initiative helped 234 families avoid displacement in 2024, with 89% maintaining housing stability after 12 months. Every dollar invested in prevention saves $7 in emergency assistance costs while keeping children in stable school environments.

Data Sources: Housing Authority, TANF Program Data, Court Records
